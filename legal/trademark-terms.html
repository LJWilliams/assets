Software Carpentry always has been, and always will be, an open project, but we do need to protect its unique identity (its "brand") to ensure that when people see our name or logo, they know what it is being used for. We want to help you to make fair use of our name and logo so we have prepared some guidelines to explain what uses are acceptable.

The "Software Carpentry" word mark and the Software Carpentry logo:

are both registered trademarks. We will refer to them together as the "Software Carpentry Marks".

1. When can I use the Software Carpentry Word Mark?
You may only use the Software Carpentry Word Mark to refer to or describe the materials we provide, materials that substantially derive from them, or training that uses our materials or materials that derive substantially derive from them and that includes substantial discussion of:
  • modular program construction,
  • task automation,
  • version control, and
  • testing.
2. When can I use the Software Carpentry Logo?
You may only use the Software Carpentry Logo in connection with training or materials as described above. If you use the Software Carpentry Logo in this way on a website, the logo must link to our website at http://software-carpentry.org.
3. When can't I use the Software Carpentry Marks?
You may not:
  1. use the Software Carpentry Marks as part of your company name, company logo or product name;
  2. use the Software Carpentry Word Mark as part of the domain name for your website (though you may use it as part of a directory path or page title within your domain, provided such use conforms to the conditions outlined in Point 1 above);
  3. use the Software Carpentry Marks in a way that creates a false impression that your training or material is endorsed by, sponsored by, or associated with Software Carpentry;
  4. manufacture, sell, or give away any items of merchandise bearing the Software Carpentry marks, such as T-shirts, caps, pens, stickers, mugs etc. (unless you have an express trade mark licence from us);
  5. use the Software Carpentry Marks in a way that shows Software Carpentry or its materials or training in a false or derogatory light;
  6. use the Software Carpentry Marks in connection with fake or counterfeit products;
  7. use a name or logo which are so similar to the Software Carpentry Marks that they may confuse or mislead people into thinking that your business, products, or services originate from or are associated with Software Carpentry;
  8. use the Software Carpentry Marks or a name or logo which are similar to them in a way that will damage our brand or reputation; or
  9. try to pass off your business, products or services as associated with Software Carpentry and/or damage our goodwill in any way.
4. What other restrictions apply to use of the Software Carpentry Marks?
Any use of the Software Carpentry Marks must be accompanied by the acknowledgement "Software Carpentry is a trademark of Software Carpentry Ltd".

No. The Software Carpentry Marks are our property. You may not apply to register the Software Carpentry Marks, whether by themselves or in combination with other words, images or devices as trade marks, designs or copyright works or any similar or analogous rights anywhere in the world.
